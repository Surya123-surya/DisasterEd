Introduction to Earthquakes

An Earthquake is the shaking of the surface of the Earth resulting from a sudden release of energy in the Earth's lithosphere. This energy release creates seismic waves that can cause damage to buildings, roads, and infrastructure.

This energy release usually happens when blocks of rock slip past one another along a fault. The point inside the Earth where the earthquake originates is the hypocenter (or focus), and the point on the Earth's surface directly above it is the epicenter

Earthquakes can occur anywhere in the world, but some regions are more prone to them due to tectonic plate boundaries. Understanding earthquakes helps us prepare and respond effectively to minimize risks and stay safe.

  • Earthquakes are measured using the Richter scale.
  • Most earthquakes are caused by movement along faults.
  • Preparedness and awareness are key to safety during an earthquake.
