Major Disasters that may occur

Comprehensive coverage of natural and man-made disasters with detailed preparation and response strategies.

Eathquakes

Caused by sudden movement of tectonic plates under the earth’s crust.
Can lead to building collapse, ground rupture, and loss of life

Floods

Occur due to heavy rainfall, dam failure, or river overflow.
Damage to agriculture, homes, and infrastructure.
Can spread waterborne diseases.

Fires

Can be natural (lightning, volcanic activity) or man-made (electrical faults, negligence).
Spread quickly and cause loss of life, property, and forest cover.
Release toxic gases, reducing air quality.

Landslides

Movement of rock, soil, or debris down a slope due to rainfall, earthquakes, or human activities.
Block roads, rivers, and damage settlements.
Can trigger secondary disasters like floods.

Cyclones

Intense tropical storms with strong winds and heavy rainfall.
Cause flooding, storm surges, and damage to infrastructure.
Long-term effects include loss of livelihood and displacement.

Tsunami

Giant sea waves caused by undersea earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, or landslides.
Inundate coastal areas, causing massive destruction and loss of life.
Secondary impacts: saltwater intrusion, contamination of drinking water.
